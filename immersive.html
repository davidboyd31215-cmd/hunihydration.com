The Science of Sweat

When you move, you lose charge

Every drop of sweat carries sodium, potassium, magnesium, and calcium out of your body. These aren’t just minerals — they’re the electrical signals your muscles need to fire.

Step 01 — The Pump

Sodium out, potassium in

The sodium-potassium pump runs in every cell, every second. Three sodium ions out, two potassium in — consuming 25% of your body’s energy just to maintain the voltage gradient that powers movement.

Step 02 — The Signal

Calcium triggers the contraction

When your brain says move, calcium floods the muscle fiber and triggers contraction. Magnesium releases the grip. Without balance between the two, cramping takes over mid-rep.
