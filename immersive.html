RECOVER

A short film about what happens after you push your limits

Act I

You gave everything.

Your muscles fired thousands of contractions. You sweated out sodium, potassium, magnesium, calcium — the ions that carry every electrical signal in your body.

Act II

The pump never stops.

Three sodium ions out. Two potassium in. The sodium-potassium pump runs in every cell, consuming 25% of your energy just to maintain the voltage that makes movement possible.

Your muscles are electrical.

Every contraction is a spark. Every recovery is a recharge.

Act III

Fiber by fiber, you rebuild.

Calcium re-enables contraction signaling. Magnesium relaxes overworked tissue and stops the cramping. Your muscle fibers knit back together, stronger than before.

This is what recovery looks like.
